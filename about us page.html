MBBS

After their education, most doctors join a hospital or specialised medical institute to improve their practical knowledge and skills. They usually work under the supervision of senior doctors at this stage and may also enrol for training or workshops to enhance specialised skills. With time and experience, they can get promotions and lead their department or even the entire hospital.

Jane Doe

physician

Primary duties: Although physicians can have different specialisations, they usually diagnose and treat a wide variety of injuries and medical conditions in patients. In most hospitals and medical institutes, they perform physical exams, prescribe medicines, analyse test results and prepare long-term treatment plans. Family physicians, emergency physicians, neurologists, radiologists, cardiologists, paediatricians and obstetricians and gynaecologists are some common types of physicians.

Johan Doe

Surgeon

Primary duties: In addition to performing surgeries and invasive procedures, surgeons and junior surgeons are a part of preoperative and postoperative patient care as well. They counsel patients, offer expert advice and maintain accurate medical records for future reference. With experience, surgeons can become chief surgeons and head an entire department of specialised surgeons and doctors during operations. Surgeons typically specialise in general surgery, neurosurgery, trauma surgery, plastic surgery and cardiothoracic surgery.
